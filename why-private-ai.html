Private AI

Why private AI
matters.

Most AI today runs on someone else's servers. Your prompts, your documents, and your data travel to a third-party data center, get processed alongside millions of other requests, and return results through infrastructure you don't control. For many use cases, that's fine. For others, it's a serious problem.

When cloud AI becomes a liability.

01
i

Compliance exposure.

Regulated industries operate under frameworks that govern where data lives and who can access it. Cloud AI vendors process data on shared infrastructure, often across jurisdictions. HIPAA, SOC 2, FedRAMP, and GDPR all impose constraints that cloud AI makes difficult or impossible to satisfy without extensive contractual and technical safeguards.

ii

Data sovereignty.

When you send a query to a cloud model, you're transmitting your proprietary data to a third party. Even with contractual assurances, you've lost physical control. For organizations handling trade secrets, patient records, classified information, or attorney-client privileged material, that loss of control is unacceptable.

iii

Vendor dependency.

Cloud AI pricing is per-token, per-seat, or per-call, and it changes at the vendor's discretion. You don't control the model versions, the deprecation schedule, or the terms of service. Building critical workflows on infrastructure someone else owns creates a dependency that compounds over time.

iv

Latency and performance.

Every cloud AI call travels over the public internet. For real-time applications like clinical decision support, financial analysis, and operational automation, that round trip introduces latency you can't eliminate. On-premises inference runs at local network speed.

What private AI looks like.

02

Private AI means models running on hardware inside your environment, whether that's your data center, your server closet, or your edge devices. Nothing leaves your network. You choose which models to deploy, how to configure them, and who has access. You control updates, retention, and governance. The tradeoff is operational complexity. Running private AI requires hardware procurement, model deployment, integration, security hardening, and ongoing management. That's where creeksea.ai comes in.

Where creeksea.ai fits.

03

From hardware selection through deployment, integration, compliance configuration, and ongoing management, we deliver private AI as a managed service. You get the security and control of on-premises infrastructure with the operational simplicity of a managed platform.
